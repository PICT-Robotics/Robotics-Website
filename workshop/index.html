PICT Robotics

PICT Robotics team takes part in the annual Asia-Pacific Robot Contest (ABU Robocon), founded in 2002 by Asia-Pacific Broadcasting Union. PICT Robotics aims to enhance the technical skills of the team members as well as gives them exposure to competitions at an international level. Moreover, the team members gain industrial experience in the fields of mechatronics and hardcore coding.

What's there for you in the Workshop

Whether you're someone who has no knowledge about the field of robotics and microcontrollers or whether you have previous knowledge and wish to learn more, you will surely have something to take from the workshop.

We will be taking you right from the basic components of a robot to being able to simulate your own Gesture Controlled Bot, Line Tracing Bot and Wall Tracing Bot.

We believe that more than just running code, it's crucial to know what you're doing and so we will be sharing our knowledge about each and every line of code you write.

Apart from coding, if you have never heard about embedded systems or even never used a breadboard, don't worry, we'll help you out in all the electronics knowledge needed to build Arduino projects.

This workshop will not only enable you in building the above mentioned Arduino projects, but also the hands-on experience of building your Arduino projects in the workshop make you capable of innovating by yourself in this ever growing field of robotics.

What will you learn?


We will take you from being a complete beginner to an intermediate learner in robotics. After participating in the bootcamp, you will be able to build your own Arduino projects as well as simulate your projects on platforms like Tinkercad.

We have been representing PICT in national-level robotics competitions like ROBOCON for the past 12 years.

Theory of Robotics

This sessiion will amke you famiiliar to all the basic concepts and components involved in building a robot. All the topics will be divided into mechanical, electronics and coding sections.

Arduino Coding

This sessiion will give you valuable knowledge in the field of Arduino coding. We will take you right from learning the basics of C and C++ to writing code for sensor interfacing with Arduino.

Real-time Project Simulation: Basics

After learning to code, time for learning to implement your code on simulation platforms. This session will give you insights about using platforms like Tinkercad to simulate simple codes for sensors.

Real-time Project Simulation: Advanced

Now, it's time to simulate a major project. This session will give you hands-on experience of simulating a major project involving multiple sensors and actuators.
